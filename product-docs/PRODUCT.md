# Workplace Assistant

## Vision

A personal knowledge system that turns the unstructured residue of a workday — meetings, conversations, decisions, commitments — into a queryable record that compounds over time.

## Problem

Knowledge workers, especially those navigating a new company or complex stakeholder environment, lose enormous context between meetings. Decisions get made and forgotten. Action items fall through the gap between note-taking apps and task managers. Organizational intelligence (who's aligned, who's blocked, what's really going on) lives entirely in memory. The tools that exist for this either require too much structure upfront (project management software) or no structure at all (raw notes). Neither surfaces the right thing at the right moment.

## Target User

A product strategy manager who is new to a large organization and needs to stay on top of commitments, organizational context, and relationship history across a dense meeting schedule — without the overhead of a formal workflow tool.

This is a single-user app. Everything is built for one person's working style.

## Value Proposition

Capture raw, unpolished notes exactly as you'd write them. Claude handles the structure: extracting who was there, what was decided, what you committed to, what's still open. The data lands in a dashboard that tells you what needs your attention today and a knowledge base you can query conversationally — months later, about things you barely remember capturing.

## Positioning

Not a task manager (no project hierarchies, no team workflows). Not a note-taking app (the raw text is an input, not the output). Not a CRM (relationship tracking is a side effect, not the primary purpose). The closest analogue is a personal intelligence layer that sits between your calendar and your brain, processing the signal from meetings and making it retrievable.
